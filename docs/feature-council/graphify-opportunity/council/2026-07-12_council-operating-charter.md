# Graphify Opportunity Council — Operating Charter

## Mandate

Determine whether any Graphify capability or concept solves a demonstrated AI Brain user problem strongly enough to justify an implementation-ready proposal. A defer or no-go decision is fully acceptable.

## Decision gates

A candidate may advance only with evidence for all eight gates:

1. Demonstrated user problem.
2. Clear AI Brain product fit.
3. Meaningful advantage over current behavior.
4. Technically feasible MVP.
5. Acceptable privacy and security posture.
6. Acceptable licensing and dependency posture.
7. Measurable outcome.
8. Clear rollback or exit strategy.

Failure of any gate requires defer, no-go, or explicit remediation before approval.

## Required rounds

### Round 1 — Independent evaluation

Specialists score all candidates without seeing other specialists' conclusions. Each record must include evidence, assumptions, confidence, a recommendation, and gate failures.

### Round 2 — Comparative debate

The coordinator publishes the recorded independent evaluations together and builds the required decision matrix. Score differences must be explained rather than averaged away.

### Round 3 — Challenge

An adversarial reviewer tests problem reality, need for Graphify, duplication, simpler alternatives, durable value, trust, privacy, operating cost, and maintenance cost.

### Round 4 — Decision

The council records go/defer/no-go, major disagreements, minority opinions, assumptions, risks, and confidence. Consensus must not be manufactured.

## Evidence rules

- Code, tests, schemas, migrations, and current configuration outrank plans and historical prototypes for AI Brain behavior.
- Graphify source and tests at a recorded SHA outrank documentation and website claims.
- Every claim is labeled as verified behavior, source claim, inference, hypothesis, recommendation, or unknown.
- Marketing claims and benchmarks remain qualified until independently reproduced.
- Graph visualization alone is not a user outcome.

## Downstream stop rule

If no candidate clears all gates, stop before feature-specific charter, PRD, UX, prototype, and technical plan. Complete the audit, research, no-go/defer record, Wiki update, and delivery package instead.
