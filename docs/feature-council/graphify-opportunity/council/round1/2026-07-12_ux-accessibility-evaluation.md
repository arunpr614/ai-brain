## Perspective-specific principles

1. A graph-shaped interface is not user value. The task must be more frequent or better completed than with current Related, selected-item Ask, grouping pages, source opening, citations, or Settings.
2. Existing-surface placement is a discoverability advantage only after users can find, understand, and use the addition without training.
3. Provenance and stale labels must communicate an actionable meaning, not merely expose implementation vocabulary. Labels, icons, color, and layout must agree programmatically and visually.
4. A nonvisual route is equal only when it supports the same decisions and actions with comparable effort. A fallback that omits canvas selection, filtering, evidence inspection, navigation, rebuild state, or no-path behavior is not parity.
5. Keyboard operability includes logical focus order, visible focus, no traps, return focus after disclosures/dialogs, and alternatives to drag, pan, pinch, and spatial-only selection.
6. Screen-reader support includes names, roles, states, headings, relationships, result counts, selection, loading, stale, error, and completion announcements without announcement flooding.
7. At 200% zoom and a 320 CSS-pixel viewport, content must reflow without clipped evidence, two-dimensional dependence, or hidden actions. Mobile must preserve the complete task, not only read-only viewing.
8. Motion must not carry meaning; animated layout, rebuild progress, or canvas transitions must respect reduced-motion preferences.
9. Empty, insufficient-evidence, error, partial, stale, no-path, and permission/consent-withdrawn states must be distinct, honest, recoverable, and privacy-safe.
10. Research itself should include participants who use keyboard, screen readers, magnification, reduced motion, and mobile. Otherwise B-00 can reproduce the same evidence gap for disabled users.

## Preferred option

**Preferred: B-00, bounded discovery/defer.** It is the only decision action that matches the present evidence quality without confusing technical adjacency or historical planning with user value. The study should compare, in task context:

1. Current Related/source opening/citations versus C-01 label-only cues and compact disclosure.
2. Current Settings versus C-02 counts/sort/filter and then, separately, cue/action concepts.
3. Current selected-item Ask/grouping/text lists versus exactly one C-03 job concept only if relationship episodes recur.
4. Completion, errors, interpretation, confidence, and effort for keyboard, screen-reader, magnification/reflow, reduced-motion, and mobile users—not merely an end-stage accessibility review.

The decision threshold should remain material task improvement over the smallest sufficient comparator. Stated preference for a graph, visual appeal, or technical feasibility is not sufficient.

## C-03 conditional variant disposition

C-03 does not pass the demonstrated-job or comparative-advantage gates, so its variants are **not peer-scored or ranked**. If later evidence reopens the family, evaluate only the smallest variant matching the observed task:

- **V-03A pair explanation:** conditional on faithful reconstruction of actual contributing signals and immediate removal of withdrawn note influence. Compare first with C-01/source opening.
- **V-03B selected-item evidence path:** conditional on a demonstrated two-item connection task, honest no-path/alternative behavior, direction/weight disclosure, and resistance to meaningless hubs or causal inference. Compare first with selected-item Ask.
- **V-03C whole-library inspector:** conditional on text-first task value plus lifecycle, scale, keyboard, screen-reader, 200%/320px reflow, reduced-motion, and mobile parity proof. An optional canvas cannot become the only route to any insight or action.

These conditions follow the packet's explicit rule that a visual interaction requires equal nonvisual completion and that Graphify's generated viewer is unsuitable ([research note](../../research/2026-07-12_graphify-research-note_v2.md#ai-brain-production-fit-criteria), [comparison](../../research/2026-07-12_ai-brain-versus-graphify-capability-comparison.md#capability-comparison)).

## Strongest contrary case

The strongest contrary case is to advance **C-01 immediately to a very small, non-production usability prototype** because its entry point already exists, it adds no graph route or persistent edge, it can represent unknown/stale state honestly, and it may be cheaper than four weeks of broad discovery. A source-kind label and current/stale indicator could reveal quickly whether users misread Related results.

I do not adopt that case in Round 1 because the packet explicitly authorizes no prototype, problem frequency is Unknown, current source opening/citations have not been shown inadequate, and even small labels can create false authority or disclose note influence. The decision-changing response is a bounded comparison inside B-00, not implementation or prototype authorization from this evaluation.

## Major assumptions and risks

- The study can recruit enough representative users within one timebox; failure to do so may merely defer the decision again.
- Current features are stable enough to serve as fair baselines, despite current runtime/provider/index health being Unknown in the audit ([audit v2](../../audit/2026-07-12_ai-brain-feature-audit_v2.md)).
- “Origin,” “current,” “stale,” “semantic,” “duplicate,” and “unused” can be translated into plain language without overstating meaning.
- Disabled users are not treated as a late conformance sample; excluding them would bias which task and interaction appears valuable.
- C-02 normalization can mistake deliberate distinctions for errors, and accessible undo cannot compensate for silent loss of manual intent.
- C-03 paths can imply causality, favor hubs, or conceal direction; no-path can be more truthful and more useful than a weak path.
- Relationship structure and grouped labels are sensitive even without raw content. Empty/error/diagnostic states can leak existence or counts.
- A visually appealing canvas can distort both usability feedback and council judgment before text-first value is established.
- The current semantic-event contract is partial and lacks durable item-deletion tombstones, so stale and deletion UX cannot be trusted without a different complete lifecycle proof ([lifecycle matrix](../../audit/2026-07-12_semantic-event-and-graph-input-lifecycle-matrix.md)).
- The risk register's open risks for novelty, duplication, misleading relationships, sensitive exposure, and large-graph accessibility remain material ([risk register](../../RISK_REGISTER.md)).

## Minimum validation that could change the recommendation

1. **Predeclare the study:** exact task, smallest comparator, task-success rubric, material-improvement threshold, privacy protocol, accessibility sampling, and one-timebox exit rule.
2. **B-00 completion evidence:** at least five comparable sessions or three natural episodes for a target job, including disability/access-mode coverage sufficient to detect unequal completion.
3. **C-01 change threshold:** recurring retrieval-trust problem plus material improvement over current source opening/citations and label-only cues; correct plain-language interpretation; zero provenance mislabeling, consent-withdrawal failure, or private telemetry leakage; keyboard/screen-reader/reflow/mobile parity.
4. **C-02 change threshold:** recurring organization-maintenance problem plus improvement over counts/sort/filter; low nuisance/dismiss rate; adversarial normalization precision; and, for every action, preview, idempotency, partial-failure recovery, undo, focus/status handling, and zero unrecoverable loss of manual intent.
5. **C-03 reopening threshold:** recurring relationship-inspection task and material improvement over C-01/current Ask/grouping/text lists. Then select one variant only. Require precision/comprehension, sensitive-association policy, complete deletion/stale/rebuild/rollback proof, representative performance, and equal keyboard/screen-reader/zoom/reflow/reduced-motion/mobile completion.
6. **State matrix for any surviving UI:** loading, empty library, no eligible evidence/relationships, no path, stale, partially stale, rebuild in progress/cancelled/failed, revoked/deleted source, authorization failure, offline/network failure, success, and rollback. Specify user copy, available action, focus destination, live announcement, privacy exposure, and mobile behavior for each.
