## Perspective-specific principles

1. **Owner records remain truth.** A similarity, label, path, or projection is derived evidence, never an authoritative memory fact.
2. **Origin is not explanation.** Showing source kind, version, or similarity says where a result came from; it does not prove why a model ranked it or that a relationship caused another fact.
3. **Uncertainty must change behavior.** Unknown, stale, weak, no-path, consent-revoked, and deleted states must be visible and must suppress stronger claims—not merely add fine print.
4. **Correction must reach every derivative.** Consent withdrawal, deletion, rename, merge, re-index, and source-quality changes must remove or refresh every affected cue, relationship, cache, path, and nonvisual view.
5. **Manual intent outranks cleanup convenience.** Exact or normalized similarity between labels is not permission to conflate them. Every mutation needs preview, idempotency, recovery, and preservation of explicit user choices.
6. **Trust is measured as correct interpretation.** Confidence or engagement alone is insufficient; users must distinguish fact, user-authored organization, model-generated label, transient similarity, and unsupported inference.
7. **A graph-shaped output is not sense-making evidence.** Knowledge-work value requires faster or more accurate recall, comparison, orientation, or correction than existing Related, selected Ask, search, citations, and grouping pages.

## Strongest contrary case

The strongest case against B-00 is that a **C-01 concept test** could itself be the smallest learning instrument: current retrieval already exists, cues could be rendered as deterministic source-kind/current-state text, and no graph or persistent relationship is required. If users demonstrably misread surprising retrieval results, waiting four weeks may prolong avoidable distrust. C-01 also creates a bounded provenance vocabulary that could improve later features without committing to a graph.

That case does not justify implementation now. The packet does not show that the trust problem recurs, that users understand the cues, that the cues beat simply opening the source/citation, or that note-derived influence can be disclosed and withdrawn safely. The contrary case therefore supports separately authorized comparative research, not a Go.

A secondary contrary case favors C-02 over C-01: exact counts and duplicate cues are deterministic and expose a direct correction affordance, whereas retrieval provenance may be technical without changing behavior. It remains weaker because cleanup frequency and mutation safety are both Unknown.

## Major assumptions and risks

- The available source metadata can distinguish original content, AI summary, manual note, user-authored collection/tag membership, model-generated organization, current index generation, and stale/unknown states without ambiguous fallback labels.
- A source/origin label will not be interpreted as proof of causal contribution. If users conflate them, even C-01's minimal cues may reduce rather than improve trust.
- Discovery participants and tasks represent real personal-memory work; five sessions or three episodes are a minimum signal, not generalizable prevalence.
- C-02 can keep read-only cues separate from mutations. If product pressure bundles them, data readiness and reversibility revert to Unknown for the whole scope.
- Manual labels that normalize to the same text can still encode intentional distinctions; deterministic detection is not deterministic correctness.
- Current single-owner auth and a short browser PIN boundary are not evidence for safe exposure of concentrated relationship summaries.
- Any relationship confidence score may be misread as truth unless its origin, evidence, version, limitations, and correction path are immediately legible.
- A no-path result can mean missing/ineligible/stale data rather than no meaningful connection; the UI must distinguish those conditions.
- Historical FCP-004 approval can create anchoring pressure. It remains a planning record with proof gates, not present authority or value evidence.
- Costs may be materially understated because privacy, lifecycle, accessibility, policy maintenance, and recovery work are not yet designed.

## Minimum validation that could change the recommendation

1. **Common discovery protocol:** Predeclare task, current-behavior baseline, correct-interpretation rubric, material improvement threshold, privacy guardrails, and stop rule. Complete at least five comparable sessions or capture at least three natural episodes for a target job during one four-week window. Do not use stated enthusiasm, graph preference, or technical adjacency as a pass.
2. **C-01:** Compare (a) current source opening/citations, (b) source-kind plus current/stale indicator only, and (c) compact disclosure. Measure correct interpretation, task completion, confidence calibration, useful follow-through, and clutter. Require zero provenance mislabeling, private telemetry leakage, and consent-withdrawal failures. A fuller explanation additionally requires faithful contribution reconstruction.
3. **C-02:** Observe natural organization-maintenance episodes and compare current Settings with counts/sort/filter before adding cues/actions. Test adversarial label pairs and every proposed mutation for preview, idempotency, interruption, undo/recovery, and preservation of explicit manual membership. Any unrecoverable intent loss is a no-go.
