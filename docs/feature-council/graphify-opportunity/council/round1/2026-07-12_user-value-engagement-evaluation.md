## 2. Perspective-specific principles and possible conflict

1. A job is decision-worthy when it recurs naturally and causes observable time, error, confidence, or follow-through harm—not merely because a capability gap or compelling visualization exists.
2. A candidate must beat both current behavior and its smallest viable comparator. Current Related, selected-item Ask, citations/source opening, grouping pages, and Settings are active comparators, even though their adoption and outcome quality are also Unknown ([audit v2](../../audit/2026-07-12_ai-brain-feature-audit_v2.md), lines 36–45; [shortlist v2](../2026-07-12_opportunity-shortlist_v2.md), lines 258–266, 366–374, and 474–483).
3. Discoverability is part of value. Existing-moment cues have a lower adoption burden than a new inspector or queue, but placement alone does not prove that people notice or understand them.
4. Success must be defined before concept exposure and measured without private titles, bodies, URLs, note text, sensitive labels, or relationship paths ([shortlist v2](../2026-07-12_opportunity-shortlist_v2.md), lines 39–48).
5. Opportunity cost is symmetric. Building an unproven feature consumes time and adds policy/maintenance burden; deferral may prolong retrieval distrust, organization friction, or synthesis difficulty if one of those problems is already material.

This lens may underweight a rare but strategically important platform foundation. I address that conflict by scoring plausible extensibility separately, but I do not convert platform adjacency, historical approval, or Graphify capability into demonstrated user value. The comparison itself warns that capability gaps are not proof of need ([AI Brain versus Graphify](../../research/2026-07-12_ai-brain-versus-graphify-capability-comparison.md), lines 53–62).

## 3. Decision summary

**Preferred decision posture: B-00 — bounded discovery / defer.** It is the only option whose immediate job is verified by the packet: reduce a consequential evidence gap under a timebox. That does not make its target product problem proven, so B-00 still cannot receive a formal `Go` under the eight-gate rule. The recommendation is to **defer product selection and execute only the bounded, privacy-safe evidence plan**, subject to predeclared thresholds and a hard exit.

The real/frequent job is not yet known. Of the feature hypotheses, C-01 is the most credible first concept to test because it intervenes in an existing high-intent retrieval moment and has the smallest adoption and implementation surface. C-02 is a reasonable second test for active curators. C-03 has the weakest current value-to-cost case: its user job, comparative advantage, adoption moment, lifecycle, and ongoing operating value are all Unknown, while its risk and cost envelope are the largest ([shortlist v2](../2026-07-12_opportunity-shortlist_v2.md), lines 419–466).

## 6. Option-specific challenge findings

### B-00 opportunity cost

B-00 is not costless. It consumes about 3–5 working days, may require four weeks elapsed, and could yield too few natural episodes to decide. If C-01's trust problem is already common, users may continue to ignore useful Related results or misread stale/note-derived signals. If C-02's problem is common, noisy organization may keep degrading findability. If C-03's job is real, synthesis friction continues. The packet offers no data to quantify any of those harms, so caution cannot be allowed to become indefinite defaulting.

The exit must be enforced: one timebox, one decision readout, and no extension unless a specifically named missing observation is likely to be decisive. Immediate no-go is the legitimate lower-cost comparator. B-00 is preferred because the uncertainty spans all product options and can plausibly be reduced cheaply—not because incomplete evidence automatically favors doing nothing ([shortlist v2](../2026-07-12_opportunity-shortlist_v2.md), lines 131–160).

### C-01 challenge

Minimal source-kind and current/stale cues must first beat current source opening/citations. A fuller explanation is prohibited unless it can reconstruct actual contributing evidence rather than narrate a centroid result after the fact. Any note-derived signal must be disclosed, owner-scoped, and removed immediately on consent withdrawal. Current evidence does not pass any of those outcome/trust tests ([shortlist v2](../2026-07-12_opportunity-shortlist_v2.md), lines 203–228 and 258–267).

### C-02 challenge

Organization noise may be real for active curators, but frequency is Unknown. Settings/attention surfaces are plausible only if users naturally visit them at cleanup time. Counts/sort/filter are the minimum comparator; a new state or action system is unjustified unless it materially beats them. Any mutation needs preview, idempotency, undo/recovery, and zero unrecoverable loss of manual intent ([shortlist v2](../2026-07-12_opportunity-shortlist_v2.md), lines 311–336 and 366–374).

### C-03 challenge

The family currently looks more like a technically rich possibility than a demonstrated frequent job. It has not beaten C-01, selected-item Ask, grouping pages, or text-only lists. Consequently no variant selection is permitted. Even later, pair explanation must faithfully reconstruct contributions; path must control hubs, direction, no-path behavior, and causal inference; inspector must pass text-first value, lifecycle, scale, mobile, and accessibility before any visual work ([shortlist v2](../2026-07-12_opportunity-shortlist_v2.md), lines 474–492).

## 7. Strongest minority / contrary case

The strongest case against B-00 is to choose a very small C-01 label/current-state change now. It is embedded in an existing high-intent surface, avoids a new route/projection, uses current metadata, has the lowest feature cost estimate, and directly addresses a trust principle central to AI Brain. A small cue could produce learning in context faster than diary research, and delaying it may perpetuate avoidable uncertainty around surprising Related results.

That case is not strong enough to pass today. The packet has no evidence that Related is used, that surprising results are frequent, that users misinterpret them, or that a badge changes correct interpretation or useful follow-through. It also explicitly disallows production instrumentation/prototyping in this round and requires the minimal cue to beat current source opening/citations. C-01 should therefore be the first concept comparator inside B-00, not a selected implementation.

## 8. Major assumptions and risks

- Participants or a representative single-owner workflow are available; recruitment does not consume more effort than the feature uncertainty warrants.
- Current surfaces are enabled and usable enough to form a fair baseline. The packet explicitly lacks fresh runtime/provider/index verification.
- A four-week diary captures naturally occurring episodes rather than prompting graph-shaped demand.
- Five task sessions or three episodes may be too weak to establish frequency. Evidence must show recurrence across people or across distinct weeks for the actual owner, not just repeated prompts in one session.
- Task confidence and useful opens are valid leading indicators of retained-memory value; they may not predict long-term adoption.
- Privacy-safe outcome recording is sufficient for decision quality without logging sensitive content.
- C-01 cue vocabulary can remain plain-language and deterministic.
- C-02 read-only value can be tested without introducing unsafe mutation.
- C-03 may have rare, high-severity value that a short study misses; diary and critical-incident prompts should preserve that possibility.
- The largest risk in B-00 is institutionalizing defer. The largest risk in C-01/C-02 is shipping low-value clutter. The largest risk in C-03 is durable cost and false authority around relationships.

## 9. Minimum validation that could change the recommendation

These are minimum decision tests, not implementation authorization. Numeric thresholds should be frozen before exposure and may be calibrated once during protocol design, never after results are seen.

### Evidence-to-decision rule

- Advance **C-01** only if retrieval-trust episodes meet V1 and the minimal cue/disclosure beats both source opening/citations and a label-only comparator under V2–V4.
- Advance **C-02** only if organization-hygiene episodes meet V1 and existing-surface cues beat counts/sort/filter under V2, with V4/V5 proving manual-intent safety.
- Reopen **C-03** only if relationship inspection itself meets V1 and materially beats C-01, selected-item Ask, grouping pages, and text lists under V2. Only then compare one smallest variant; variant-specific V4–V12 remain mandatory.
- End at **no-go/defer** if the timebox yields insufficient natural evidence, current behavior performs equivalently, or any required trust/privacy/exit gate remains Unknown.

## 10. Final recommendation and authorization boundary

Choose **B-00 as the preferred bounded decision posture**, not as evidence that users lack a problem. Run one privacy-safe, prethresholded timebox with current behavior plus minimum-change concept comparisons, then decide. Test C-01 first, C-02 second if curator episodes appear, and C-03 only at the family-job level. On current evidence: B-00, C-01, and C-02 are **Defer**; C-03 is **No-go for implementation selection in this round** and its historical active scope should remain deferred rather than silently surviving as a parallel plan.
